## Very Large Array (Another Drive Day)
Today was planned to be a driving day with a short stop off to see a few antennas to satisfy Dad's nerd gene. After just over three hours of driving, we stopped to see the [Very Large Array (VLA)](http://www.vla.nrao.edu/) which is a radioscopic telescope used to measure changes deep into space and to produce scientific visualizations. The scale of this installation was truly impressive... 27 antennas spread in a "y" pattern with each leg extending up to 13 miles. To get a feel for the size of these things, look at the first picture below and you will see two people standing at the base. Our short little stop turned into two hours of walking around, viewing the information video, reading, etc. We were even able to have our lunchtime picnic there in the shadow of the array.

After leaving there, we drove on to Holbrook, AZ for our second night in a "motel" (the kids think these are *so* much better than boring hotels). Tomorrow is a relatively easy day with a visit to the Petrified Forest and a stop at a road-side museum as we head to Williams, AZ.
