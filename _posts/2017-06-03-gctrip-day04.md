## The Alamo!
Today was a great day. We knew it was going to be an easy drive day, so we started off slow and just relaxed. Leaving Houston around 9:30, we stopped for lunch around noon at a wonderful rest stop complete with a playground. Once again we enjoyed the picnic and then let the kids run around for a bit. Got back on the road a bit after 1 and were at the Alamo around 2 in the afternoon.

I must say, I've only been to the Alamo once before and it was in the middle of the night on a school trip (I don't remember much) but it was much smaller than I expected/remembered. In spite of that, it was a great stop and we learned quite a bit about the history of the site, the Republic of Texas, and its transformation into the State of Texas. We toured the site for just under two hours and all of us enjoyed ourselves.

From there, we walked to the "Riverwalk" - an indcredible feature of downtown San Antonio that includes pathways, parks, shops and resturants along a couple of miles of river running among the buildings up at street level. We walked around, had some ice cream, saw a suprising number of wedding parties (including a bride who entered the back of the ice cream shop and started serving her friends and family - while wearing her wedding dress). We continued wandering until we stopped at a river-side resturant for dinner (The Iron Cactus - quite good) and then headed to the grocery store and hotel for the night. Tomorrow is a long drive day... today was a great lead up to that.
