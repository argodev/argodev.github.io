## Horsehoe Bend/Monument Valley
Today - day 10 - is the longest day of our trip. I'm exhausted so this will likely be a shorter post. We started out early from Williams, AZ and drove to Horseshoe Bend. Because of the way our hotels fell into place (availability), this was supposed to be about 8 hours of driving, not counting meals or the two stops we had planned. The trip over to Horseshoe Bend took a little over two hours and the visit was very enjoyable. We got there around 10:30 - it was already very busy, hot, and incredibly windy. The path from the parking lot to the bend and back is about 1.2 miles of hiking up and down a sandy hill. The sand, combined with the wind caused some issues, but otherwise it was great. The view was classic and would only have been better if it were sunrise or sunset.  

Most of what made today so long was the fact that you really can't get there from here... at least not on an interstate. We spend most all day on two lane roads (with the occasional passing lane). The drive was beautiful, and we got to see some incredible parts of the country, but it wasn't a fast drive. The wind was blowing fairly hard most all day which resulted in a brownish sky and also created some challenges around lunch. Since we weren't on an interstate, there weren't rest stops, and - frankly - there wasn't anything much at all between Horseshoe Bend and Monument Valley. We eventually stopped on the side of the road at the entrance to a farm road and Julie and I made lunches and handed them up to the kids to eat in the car (far too windy to eat outside... paper plates/napkins would have been everywhere). 

Monument Valley is located on Indian land and is therefore not a national park. We paid to for the privelege of driving the 17-mile loop and that was quite an experience. When these folks say "rough road", they weren't joking. You didn't exactly need a 4WD vehicle, but it would have helped. I think it is going to take years to get that red dirt out of the Suburban. Once we got to the sites, however, the views were incredible and very much worth the stop.

We wound up in Farmington, NM around 8PM local time and, after getting a bite to eat, got the kids settled around 9:30. Thankfully, tomorrow is a shorter day - a drive over to Albuquerque and a stop at the National Museum of Nuclear Science & History. We are pretending that this visit is for the kids, and we have even gotten a few of them excited about it, but in all honesty, I think I am the most excited.
